About Us

I’m Salatiso Lonwabo Mdeni, and my greatest joy is being a father to my son, Sazi. Everything I do—whether it’s building this website, writing books, or sharing stories—is to give Sazi and his cousins a connection to our Xhosa heritage. Born in 1982 in Transkei, South Africa, I grew up in Fort Malan and Nqabane, surrounded by the rolling hills and the rhythm of Xhosa songs, stories, and proverbs. After my father’s death in 1993, my mother raised me and my siblings with her unwritten wisdom and the strength of our community. That’s where I learned the power of family, and it’s why I created *Saz' isiXhosa, no Sapho*—“Sazi speaks Xhosa, with the Family”—to ensure Sazi, now seven, and his cousins grow up knowing who they are.

This isn’t just my project—it’s a family affair. Sazi’s curiosity lights the way, asking questions that spark our lessons. His cousins—my nephews aged 12, 8, and 4, and nieces aged 6 and 8 months—bring playfulness, testing quizzes and giggling over words like “Molo” (Hello). Their grandmother, my mother, shares her unwritten wisdom, stories of Transkei gatherings that we’re now writing down for the first time. My sisters, the aunts, add their knowledge, from Xhosa traditions to everyday phrases. I provide the platform and guidance, drawing on my 20 years in risk management, property entrepreneurship, and authorship to make this a space where our family’s heritage thrives.

My motivation comes from Sazi’s smile when he learns a new Xhosa word, but it’s bigger than us. In South Africa, Xhosa is at risk—schools often focus on global languages, and modern life pulls families apart. I’ve seen this in Johannesburg, where I’ve built my life, and in the challenges I’ve faced to stay present for Sazi, from legal battles to advocating for family-led education. This website is my promise to Sazi, his cousins, and every family: a fun, interactive place to learn Xhosa together, strengthening bonds and preserving our culture. My books, like Zazi Mzantsi Afrika and The Homeschooling Father, and my YouTube channel share this culture freely—because it’s about empowering families, not profit.

Through *Saz' isiXhosa, no Sapho*, we’re writing our family’s story—Sazi’s first words, his grandmother’s tales, his cousins’ laughter, and the love that ties us together. Join us to learn Xhosa, share your own stories, and keep our heritage alive for the next generation.
