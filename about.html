About Us

I’m Salatiso Lonwabo Mdeni, a father to my son Sazi, and this website is my promise to him. Growing up in Transkei’s Fort Malan, I was surrounded by Xhosa songs and stories, shaped by my mother’s wisdom after my father’s passing. Now, I’m building *Saz’ isiXhosa, no Sapho*—Sazi speaks Xhosa, with the Family—to share that heritage with Sazi, his cousins, and our whole family.

It’s a family affair: Sazi’s curiosity leads us, his cousins add laughter, their grandmother shares tales we’re writing down, and aunts bring their knowledge. I guide the way, creating a place for us to learn Xhosa together. Want to hear more about our journey and why we’re doing this?

Born in 1982, I grew up in a Transkei community where Xhosa was the heartbeat of every gathering. After losing my father in 1993, my mother raised us with strength and stories, teaching me the power of family. Today, living in Johannesburg, I see Sazi and his cousins—nephews aged 12, 8, and 4, nieces aged 6 and 8 months—facing a world that pulls them from their roots. Schools often skip Xhosa for global languages, and modern life challenges family time. That’s why I created this site: to give Sazi and his cousins a fun, interactive way to learn Xhosa, while bringing our family closer.

My work as an author and advocate fuels this mission. Books like Zazi Mzantsi Afrika and The Homeschooling Father share Xhosa culture and family values, inspired by my own journey—from legal battles to ensure I could be there for Sazi, to 20 years in risk management solving problems. My mother’s unwritten wisdom, once shared under Transkei skies, is now being written here, with Sazi’s grandmother and aunts adding their voices. Through this site and my YouTube channel, we’re keeping Xhosa alive for Sazi, his cousins, and families everywhere, one word at a time.
