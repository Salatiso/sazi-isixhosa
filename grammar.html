Grammar

Join Salatiso and Sazi’s family in learning Xhosa grammar! Understanding grammar helps Sazi and his cousins tell their grandmother’s stories in Xhosa, connecting our family through language. Choose a category below to explore, or try a quiz to test your skills!

Xhosa sentences usually follow a Subject-Verb-Object order, like “I speak Xhosa.” Try these examples!

English Xhosa Action Pronunciation Cultural Note
I speak Xhosa Ndithetha isiXhosa
Sazi uses this to share stories!
The child eats Umntwana uyadla
Like Sazi enjoying a meal.

Xhosa nouns have classes, like um- for one person or aba- for many. Try these examples!

English Xhosa Action Pronunciation Cultural Note
Person Umntu
Class 1, one person like Sazi.
People Abantu
Class 2, our family community.
