Inkqubo Yenkcubeko (Cultural Practices)

Sazi’s family practices like *Imbeleko* welcome babies, just like we welcomed his 8-month-old cousin! Explore Xhosa traditions with Sazi and his cousins to learn how we honor our Transkei roots. Choose a category or try a quiz to test your knowledge!

These practices mark important moments in life, from birth to marriage, bringing our family together.

Name Description When Performed Pronunciation
Birth of a Child Ukuzalwa Komntwana
A significant event for family and community, celebrating and naming the child, with cleansing rituals. Isiganeko esibaluleke kakhulu kwintsapho nakuluntu. Iinkcubeko ezahlukeneyo zilandela ukuzalwa, njengokubhiyozela nokunika umntwana igama. After the birth of a child. Emva kokuzalwa komntwana.
Welcoming a Newborn Imbeleko
Protecting and formally introducing a newborn to the community, often with a small feast. Umntwana osanda kuzalwa ukhuselwa kwaye waziswa ngokusemthethweni kuluntu. In the first weeks or months of a child’s life. Kwiiveki okanye iinyanga zokuqala zobomi bomntwana.

These traditions shape our daily lives, teaching respect and community.

Name Description When Performed Pronunciation
Respect Intlonipho
Respecting elders is vital, shown through speech, attitude, and actions. Ukuhlonipha abantu abadala kubaluleke kakhulu. Throughout life. Ubomi bonke.

These practices honor our ancestors and guide us through loss.

Name Description When Performed Pronunciation
Mourning Ukuzila
When someone passes, the family and community mourn to show grief and respect. Xa kufiwe, intsapho eshiyekileyo kunye noluntu bayazila. After someone’s death. Emva kokufa komntu.
